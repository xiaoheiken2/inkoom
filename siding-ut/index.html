Siding Installation Process


The siding installation process involves several essential steps to ensure a flawless and high-performance outcome. First, the existing siding must be stripped away, and the underlying structure inspected and prepped. Insulation layers and moisture barriers are then added to guard your home.

Next, the siding materials are allowed to acclimate for 10–14 days, helping to prevent warping due to weather conditions. After that, the siding is fitted with expert accuracy, with close attention to details like trim and vents.

Choosing a professional for installation has many advantages over DIY attempts. Trained professionals have the right experience to work efficiently and safely, saving you time and money. They also have access to high-quality materials and can recommend the best options for your specific home.

By sticking to proven methods, professional installers ensure correct ventilation, protection from water, and solid structural performance. This not only elevates your home's appearance, but also protects it from weather-related wear.

Expert Siding Fixes

Siding can endure wear and tear from constant exposure with the elements, which may lead to cracks, punctures, or detached panels. For loose or detached siding panels, our team follows a thorough process to reattach them properly. We begin by diagnosing the root cause—often moisture damage. Then, we re-secure the panels using durable fasteners and techniques for a secure fit that stands up to the elements.

No matter the issue, our experienced team strives to minimize disruption while delivering repairs that last and keep your siding both strong and visually appealing.

Eco-Friendly Composite Siding


Composite siding, also referred to as engineered wood, is a modern choice for homeowners interested in a low-maintenance and modern siding option. Blended using wood fibers and plastic polymers, it combines excellent durability with aesthetic flexibility.

A key advantage of composite siding is its sustainable composition. In contrast to traditional vinyl, which may release harmful chemicals like harmful emissions when exposed to fire or extreme heat, composite siding is made from recycled content and does not contain toxic additives. Its durability also reduces waste.

In addition to its green credentials, composite siding is available in a diverse range of styles and finishes, mimicking the appearance of other popular siding types. This customization potential makes it easy to achieve their desired look while enjoying the benefits of engineered construction.

In terms of maintenance, composite siding is virtually worry-free. It resists common siding issues, maintaining a beautiful appearance year after year. Unlike wood, it eliminates the need for frequent painting or staining, reducing upkeep costs.
